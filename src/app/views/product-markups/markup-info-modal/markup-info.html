Prices are managed through markups: A markup is a monetary amount added to the existing price of a product to calculate what price is shown to a customer.

A markup can be added through the following scenarios: - The entire application marketplace - All products and customers - An individual customer - Any product for a single customer - An individual product: A single product for all customers

- A combination of product and customers: A specific customer purchasing a specific product A markup is never placed atop another markup, to avoid complex pricing. Markups are applied as follows: 

1. A markup applied to specific products and customers take first priority: When a markup is applied to a specific product and customer, activated during purchase, all other markups impacting the specified product will be ignored. (Case 2, 3 & 4)

2. Markups applied to a specific customer are second in priority When no markup is specified for a customer + product (As in Case 1), but a markup is specified for a particular customer, it will be applied to all purchases by this customer. Any other markups impacting the customer or product will be ignored (Case 3 & 4).

3. Markups applied to a specific product are third in priority

When a markup is specified for a particular product, it will be applied for any customers purchasing it who don’t already have a customer specific markup (Case 1 & 2). General markups, as shown in case 4, will be ignored.

4. General markups have the lowest priority When no markups are specified on a customer or product level, any markups attached to the entire marketplace will be applied to customers purchasing any product.
